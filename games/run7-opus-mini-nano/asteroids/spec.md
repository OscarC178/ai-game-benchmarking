# Game Spec: Asteroids

## 1. Overview
Asteroids (Atari, 1979) — classic vector-style shooter. Pilot a triangular ship in a wrapping play area, shoot and dodge asteroids that split into smaller pieces when hit. Features inertial physics (thrust-based movement with momentum) and screen-edge wrapping. Clean vector-line aesthetic on black.

## 2. Canvas & Rendering
- Canvas size: 800×600px
- Background color: #000000
- Frame rate: 60 FPS
- Coordinate system: origin top-left
- All objects rendered as outlined polygons (stroke, no fill) for vector aesthetic

## 3. Game Objects

### Player Ship
- **Shape:** Isosceles triangle, 3 vertices defined relative to center: nose (0, −18), left (−12, 12), right (12, 12)
- **Size:** 24×30px bounding box
- **Color:** #FFFFFF stroke, 2px line width
- **Starting position:** x=400, y=300 (center)
- **Movement:** Rotation (left/right) at 270°/s. Thrust adds acceleration in facing direction at 200px/s². Max speed 350px/s. Friction: velocity *= 0.99 per frame. Screen wraps on all edges.
- **Thrust visual:** When thrust active, draw a small flickering triangle behind ship (exhaust flame) in #FF8800

### Asteroids
- **Shape:** Irregular polygon (7–10 vertices generated by random radius variation ±30% from base radius, stored on spawn)
- **Sizes:** Large = 40px radius, Medium = 20px radius, Small = 10px radius
- **Color:** #AAAAAA stroke, 1.5px line width
- **Starting count:** 4 large asteroids (wave 1), +1 per wave
- **Starting positions:** Random, but at least 150px from player
- **Movement:** Random direction, speed: Large 50–80px/s, Medium 80–120px/s, Small 120–180px/s. Screen wraps.

### Player Bullets
- **Shape:** Small circle, 2px radius
- **Color:** #FFFFFF fill
- **Speed:** 500px/s in ship's facing direction at time of fire
- **Lifetime:** 0.8 seconds, then disappear
- **Limit:** Max 4 on screen

### UFO (Bonus Enemy)
- **Shape:** Classic saucer — horizontal ellipse 32×12px with a 20×8px dome on top (draw as 2 overlapping ellipses)
- **Color:** #FF4444 stroke, 1.5px
- **Appears:** Every 20–30s (random). Moves horizontally at 120px/s, wraps vertically. Fires aimed bullets at player every 1.5s.
- **UFO bullet:** 2px radius, #FF4444, 350px/s toward player position

## 5. Game Rules & Logic

1. Ship rotates and thrusts with inertia. No instant-stop — momentum carries.
2. All objects wrap around screen edges (exit right → enter left, etc.).
3. Shooting a large asteroid splits it into 2 medium. Medium → 2 small. Small → destroyed.
4. Clearing all asteroids starts the next wave: +1 large asteroid, all spawn away from player.
5. Player has 3 lives. Contact with asteroid or UFO bullet = death. On death, ship respawns at center after 2s if area is clear (no asteroids within 100px).
6. Player is briefly invulnerable for 2s after respawning (ship blinks at 8Hz).
7. Game over at 0 lives.

## 6. Collision Detection

- Player Bullet ↔ Asteroid: split/destroy asteroid, destroy bullet
- Player Bullet ↔ UFO: destroy UFO, destroy bullet, +200 points
- Player Ship ↔ Asteroid: player dies (unless invulnerable)
- Player Ship ↔ UFO Bullet: player dies (unless invulnerable)
- UFO Bullet ↔ Asteroid: destroy bullet (no asteroid damage)

Use circular collision detection (distance between centers < sum of radii).

## 7. Scoring

- Starting score: 0
- Score display: top-left at (10, 30), #FFFFFF, 18px monospace
- Large asteroid: 20 points
- Medium asteroid: 50 points
- Small asteroid: 100 points
- UFO: 200 points
- Extra life at 10,000 points
- High score: localStorage, top-right

## 8. UI Elements

- **Score:** top-left 18px white monospace
- **Lives:** top-left below score, show small ship icons (12px triangles)
- **High Score:** top-right 16px
- **Game Over screen:** "GAME OVER" centered 40px #FFFFFF. Score. "Press Enter to Restart" 18px #AAAAAA. Semi-transparent overlay.
- **Start screen:** "ASTEROIDS" centered y=220, 52px #FFFFFF monospace. A few decorative asteroids drifting in background. "Press Enter to Start" y=360, 20px #AAAAAA.
- **Pause:** "PAUSED" centered 36px white on #000000CC overlay.

## 9. Audio (Optional / Bonus)
- Thrust: low rumble 80Hz sawtooth while key held
- Fire: short 880Hz triangle, 30ms
- Asteroid explode: white noise burst, 80ms (louder for larger)
- Player death: descending noise 300ms
- UFO: oscillating 440–660Hz while alive

## 10. Implementation Notes

1. Screen wrapping: when any object's position exceeds canvas bounds, wrap to opposite edge. For large objects, draw on both edges simultaneously during transition.
2. Inertial physics are core to the feel — ship must coast with momentum. Friction 0.99/frame gives gradual slowdown.
3. Asteroid shapes must be generated randomly on spawn and stored — don't regenerate each frame (flickering).
4. When splitting asteroids, the 2 children should fly apart at ±30° from the bullet's direction.
5. Respawn safety check: don't respawn if any asteroid is within 100px of center.
